Correlations Discovered between Health Risks, Age, and Income

There appears to be a positive correlation between people in poverty and: An absence of healtcare coverage, obesity, and smoking.

Inversely, increasing income (coming out of poverty) has a negative correlation with smoking, obesity, and absence of healthcare.

Additionaly, age shows a distinct positive relationship with smoking, as smoking decreases.The correlation between age and obesity (and any metric other than poverty), appear to be inconclusive.
